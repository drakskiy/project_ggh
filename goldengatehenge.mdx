In and around UC Berkeley, there are many popular sunset viewpoints that offer great views of the Golden Gate Bridge, but less well known is the opportunity to see what we call “Golden Gate Henge” — not to be confused with California Henge. This effect occurs when the sun appears to set between the bridge’s two towers, a phenomenon unique to our stretch of the East Bay.

Golden Gate Henge relies on the Earth’s tilted axis, which causes the sun’s rising and setting points to drift north during the spring and south during the fall. This means that the sun only aligns with the bridge for a limited portion of the year — roughly mid-September to mid-March.

We created a site featuring an interactive map offering a collection of locations where one can catch a nice sunset and also see Golden Gate Henge during certain times of the year. The map uses solar position and the Earth’s geometry to estimate when Golden Gate Henge can be seen at a given location. For each of the pre-picked locations, we added ratings out of five based on three criteria — accessibility, views and environment — along with a weighted overall rating prioritizing views and environment.

https://drakskiy.github.io/project_ggh/

If you are feeling more adventurous and would like to find your own secret spot to see Golden Gate Henge, the map allows users to select any date in the year with the slider and displays potential locations for that date via a gold band that shifts northward nearing winter solstice and southward nearing summer solstice.

Note that between mid-March and mid-September, the gold band will not be visible as Golden Gate Henge does not occur. Additionally, even though some locations may be within the gold band, the sunset may be obstructed by landmarks such as hills, trees and buildings, especially further into the Berkeley Hills, so it is best to consult street view imagery to ensure there is a clear view of the bridge.

For the best results, aim for the middle of the map window we’ve provided. In general, the best spots are located on the western side of the Berkeley Hills as they are elevated above the city and are less likely to be obstructed. Clicking on your preferred location on the map will create a dropped pin displaying the sunset time in Pacific Time on your selected date.

Weather conditions are equally important. High clouds enhance a sunset, while low-level clouds are more likely to block the sun entirely. Especially with the Bay Area’s climate, marine layers off the coast are very common and could block the sun before it can be level with the bridge.

However, if there is even a sliver of clear sky between the horizon and the cloud layer, it could be worth the shot — sometimes the best sunsets come on cloudy days. Since conditions change quickly, it’s worth using tools like <a href="https://www.yr.no/">Yr.no</a> and <a href="https://www.windy.com/">Windy</a> to repeatedly check forecasts, particularly cloud cover, leading up to sunset.
